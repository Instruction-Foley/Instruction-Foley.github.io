Instruction Foley: Multi-Instruction Foley for Video to Audio Generation

Anonymous

Vanilla Foley v.s Instruction-Foley: (a) Vanilla Foley, despite being a common foley manner, produces the entire audio only from video inputs once, demonstrating limitations in controllability and flexibility. (b) INSTRUCTION-FOLEY, in contrast, offers a more appealing alternative that employs multiple instructions to produce high-quality synchronized audio and can produce mixed audio in multi-stages, thereby exhibiting greater practical application.
